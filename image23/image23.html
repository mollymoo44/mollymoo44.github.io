Lundy Island is a island off the shore of Westward Ho! It has a small area of about
1,100 acres but dispite the space restrictions manages to comfortably harbour a small village
and a church. Lundy Island's church stands just outside the village and built by the very first
inhabitant of the island makes it one of the oldest standing structures on the island. The small
village on Lundy has a decent sized shop with everyday items as well as souvenirs for visitors, it
also has many houses and a pub that serves all kinds of drinks foods and not to mention its
7.00 pound chips. There is a ferry going from Bideford to the island that allows people to have a
scenic boat ride and spend a day exploring the island and viewing its interesting ecosystem.
Exciting animals can often be seen on the island such as puffins and sharks making it the ideal
place for birdwatchers and animal enthusiasts alike.
